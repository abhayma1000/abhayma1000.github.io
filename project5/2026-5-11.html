Navigation

Got to learn a bit about navigation, filter theory, a little about liner and non-linear systems

Key takeaways:

  • For every estimate or measurement, there is some uncertainty, that can approximately be modeled as a Gaussian distribution. KFs are a real-time optimal way to combine these estimates
  • Not every system is fully observable or controllable. For R^3, obsevability is simple, but for objects like quaternions, it's more complex
  • For non-linear systems, we can linearize about a certain point for dynamics propagation and measurements. Introduce some error there, but works well enough for small time steps and small uncertainties (small-angle approx.)
  • For systems for example position and orientation are tightly coupled, undcertainties in one propagate to the other and depending on mission requirements, might have to lose mathematical accuracy
