Overview

For 2.5 years in high school, worked on model rockets

#d Printed parts and integrated electronics like microcontroller, altimeter, GPS, gyroscope

Details

Started with fully 3D printed rocket running off of an A engine. Anyone with a brain cell knows that the heavy weight of additive manufactured plastic combined with a weak engine means the rocket goes nowhere

I tried custom solutions for body, nose cone, parachute, fins, etc.

I iteratively got rid of custom parts for more off-shelf and lighter components and increased the engine strength and started getting pretty good heights

Iterated through different nosecones, trying different aerosynamic profiles and simulating through OpenRocket before deploying. Also iterated through many custom parts to hold the engine in place

Started putting electronics in the bay and 3D printing custom solutions to hold them in (got pretty difficult)

Able to get GPS, Raspberry pi pico, altimeter, gyroscope, and RF module to communicate to ground station

What I learned

Instead of vertically integrating first, horizontally integrate first and slowly vertically integrate. However for learning purposes, glad I tried to do everything first

Test everything. I remember testing the sensors and parachute by just throwing it off my balcony. Saves so much time rather than going to launch, realizing it doesn't work, and not knowing why

Test at different stages. Test everything layed out, test by just dropping it, test in a rocket and dropping, then test the real thing. Test different stages
